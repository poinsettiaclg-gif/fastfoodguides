The Flying Dutchman is a staple of the In-N-Out secret menu. It is simply two slices of melted American cheese sandwiched between two beef patties. No buns, no veggies, no spread.

## Why The Kitchen Loves It
Unlike the monstrous 4x4s or complex animal-style fry modifications, the Flying Dutchman is a breeze for the Board worker to assemble. Because there is no bun to toast and no produce to dress, it actually *saves* time on the assembly line. The grill cook simply throws the cheese on the patties, stacks them, and slides them into the clam-shell packaging.

## The Pro Tip
If you want to elevate this without ruining the kitchen's flow, ask for "Grilled Onions" on your Flying Dutchman. The grill cook will sandwich a scoop of caramelized onions between the two cheese slices. It takes them zero extra time since the onions are already hot on the grill, but it drastically improves the flavor.
