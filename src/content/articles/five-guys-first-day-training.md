Five Guys is loud. That is usually the first thing new hires notice when they walk in for orientation. Between the music, the constant callouts from the grill, and the rhythmic thumping of the fry shakers, it is an environment built on constant, high-energy movement.

If you are preparing for your first day at Five Guys, you might be intimidated by the open-kitchen concept where customers watch your every move. But over my decade in QSR management, I've found that Five Guys actually has one of the most structured onboarding pipelines in the fast-casual burger sector. They do not throw you to the wolves on day one. 

Here is exactly what you can expect during your initial training period.

## Phase 1: Five Guys University and Administration

Your first shift will almost entirely consist of administration and digital learning. You will usually sit in the back office or a quiet corner of the dining room. 

*   **The Paperwork:** As with any corporate job, you will fill out I-9 verifications, direct deposit forms, and acknowledge the employee handbook. You will also receive your uniform: a red shirt, an apron, and a hat. You must provide your own slip-resistant shoes.
*   **Five Guys University:** This is the core of their digital onboarding. You will watch a series of mandatory training videos that cover the brand's history, customer service expectations, and operational standards.
*   **Food Safety Quizzes:** Pay close attention during the videos. Five Guys takes food safety exceptionally seriously, especially given their commitment to fresh ingredients. You will be quizzed on cross-contamination, handwashing frequency, and allergen protocols. These modules are strictly enforced to align with the [FDA's Food Code standards](https://www.fda.gov/food/retail-food-protection/fda-food-code) for retail food establishments.
*   **The Culture Check:** Five Guys emphasizes a culture of "continuous movement." You will learn that if there are no burgers to make, there are potatoes to wash, floors to sweep, or peanuts to restock.

## Phase 2: The Physical Tour

Once the digital modules are complete, a manager or designated trainer will take you on a physical walkthrough of the restaurant. 

Because Five Guys does not use freezers (a major point of pride for the brand), their walk-in coolers are massive and highly organized. You will learn:

*   The location of the prep sinks where the potatoes are washed and cut.
*   The raw meat staging area and how it is strictly separated from produce.
*   The flow of the main line, starting from the bun toaster down to the fry station.
*   Where the cleaning chemicals are stored (and why they are kept far away from food prep areas).

## Phase 3: Getting Your Hands Dirty

Depending on how long your orientation shift is, you may step onto the line toward the end of your first day or the beginning of your second. 

Five Guys operates on a highly specific station progression. Management rarely puts a brand-new hire on the grill or the fry station. Those roles dictate the pace of the entire restaurant and require muscle memory you simply don't have yet. 

Instead, you will likely start in one of two places:

### The Register and Lobby
If you have strong customer service skills, you may start on the register. You will learn the POS system, which is relatively straightforward since the menu is intentionally limited. The hardest part of the register is learning the specific terminology for calling out orders to the grill (e.g., "Two patties, all the way!"). When not taking orders, you will be expected to conduct "lobby sweeps," wiping down tables, checking trash cans, and ensuring the peanut boxes are full.

### The Dressing/Topping Station
If you are starting on the line, you will almost certainly start at the dressing station. This is where the open-kitchen concept becomes real. 

Your trainer will shadow you as you learn the exact order and quantity of toppings for each burger. Five Guys has a very specific build order (e.g., mayo and ketchup on the bottom bun, lettuce and tomato on the top bun) to ensure the burger maintains its structural integrity. You will learn what "All The Way" actually means (mayo, lettuce, pickles, tomatoes, grilled onions, grilled mushrooms, ketchup, and mustard) and how to wrap the finished product tightly in foil. 

## The Reality of the Job

The biggest hurdle for new hires at Five Guys isn't the complexity of the menu—it's the physical demand. 

You are going to be standing, moving, wiping, and calling out orders for your entire shift. The open kitchen means you cannot hide in the back if you are feeling overwhelmed; you are essentially on stage. 

However, this transparency also creates a strong team dynamic. Because everyone is working shoulder-to-shoulder in full view, the communication has to be excellent. If you show up with slip-resistant shoes, a willingness to be loud when calling orders, and the energy to keep moving even when there are no tickets on the board, you will fit in perfectly.
