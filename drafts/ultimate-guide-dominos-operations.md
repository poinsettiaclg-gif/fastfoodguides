Running a Domino's franchise is less about cooking and more about managing an intense logistical network. While other fast-food restaurants only have to worry about the customers in their drive-thru, a Domino's store is constantly tracking dozens of drivers across a delivery radius while simultaneously managing massive spikes in online orders.

Everything in the store is dictated by data, speed, and spatial awareness. The kitchen is designed for linear flow: from the dough table to the makeline, into the oven, and out to the dispatch screen.

This guide serves as your central hub for understanding the mechanics of a high-volume Domino's operation.

## The Digital Brain: The Pulse System

You cannot understand Domino's without understanding their proprietary POS and logistics software: **Pulse**. 

Pulse handles the entire lifecycle of a pizza. The moment a customer clicks "Order" on their phone, the pie appears on the makeline screen. The system calculates prep times, tracks oven capacity, and assigns the finished order to a specific driver based on geographical routing algorithms.

## The Makeline: Dough Stretching

The physical creation of the pizza begins at the dough table. Domino's uses fresh dough that arrives in refrigerated commissary trays. It is a living product that requires careful proofing.

Unlike [Little Caesars](/articles/chain/little-caesars) which uses a mechanical sheet-out machine to flatten their crusts, Domino's pies are hand-stretched to order. The "Dough Slapper" must master a specific technique of edge-stretching and tossing to push the air to the crust and create the perfect circle. 

If the dough is stretched incorrectly, the center will be paper-thin and tear in the oven. Read about the physical toll and technique in our guide to [Domino's Dough Stretching](file:///C:/Users/Poins/.gemini/antigravity/scratch/fastfoodguides.com/src/content/articles/dominos-dough-stretching.md).

## The Bottleneck: The Oven Tender

Once the pizza is topped, it is pushed into a massive, multi-tiered conveyor belt oven. The oven is a set-it-and-forget-it machine, taking exactly 6.5 to 7 minutes to bake a pizza. 

However, the end of that oven is the most stressful position in the entire store: The Oven Tender.

During a Friday night dinner rush, pizzas pour out of the oven in a continuous, relentless stream. The Oven Tender stands at the catch tray with a pizza peel, and they must slice, box, and label every single item before the next one falls off the belt onto the floor. If the Oven Tender falls behind, the entire store grinds to a halt. ## Delivery and Logistics

Once the pizza is boxed, it hits the routing station. Drivers rely on the dispatch screen to group their deliveries geographically for maximum efficiency. 

But managing cash flow for drivers is a massive security and operational concern. Drivers are not allowed to carry large amounts of cash to deter robbery, which is why the chain enforces strict cash drop policies.
